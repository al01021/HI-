Nature and Wildlife

around Hayling Island


Langstone Harbour Nature Reserve

One of the best places to spot seal pups in the UK, from late November to early January and home to around fifty seals throughout the year. Langstone Harbour is a Site of Special Scientific Interest and a Special Protection area and tens of thousands of seabirds can be found around this stunning coastline.

West Hayling Local Nature Reserve

Oyster fishing has been practiced on Hayling Island since Roman times and the Oyster Beds are now home to a huge number of water-birds both resident and migratory. In addition, Kestrels, Hawks, Little Owls and Buzzards are also some of the many birds of prey that can be spotted at various times of the year.

Hayling Billy Coastal Path

Following the bed of the old railway line between Havant and Hayling Island, the Hayling Billy Coastal Path is a track used by walkers, cyclists and horse riders and is a fantastic way to spend a morning or afternoon to view the huge array of fauna and flora both inland and along our magnificent coastal waters.
